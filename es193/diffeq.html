Differential Equations

First Order

A first order differential equation has dxdy​ as it's highest derivative. For the two methods below, it is important the equation is in the correct form specified.

Seperating Variables

For an equation of the form dxdy​=f(x)g(y)

The solution is ∫g(y)dy​=∫f(x)dx

Integrating Factors

For an equation of the form dxdy​+P(x)y=Q(x)

An integrating factor μ can be found such that: μ(x)=e∫P(x)dx

Multiplying through by μ gives

μ(x)dxdy​+μ(x)P(x)y=μ(x)Q(x)

Then, applying he product rule backwards gives a solution:

dxd​(μ(x)y)=μ(x)Q(x) μ(x)y=∫μ(x)Q(x)dx

Second Order

A second order ODE has the form: adx2d2y​+bdxdy​+cy=f(x)

The equation is homogeneous if f(x)=0.

The auxillary equation is ak2+bk+c=0

This gives two roots k1​ and k2​, which determine the complementary function:

The particular integral is found using a trial solution, then substituting it into the equation to find the coefficients.
